CSE 101 Prerequisites:
CSE 101 requires CSE 12, CSE 16, CSE 13S, CSE 30, and MATH 19B or MATH 20B.

Upper-Division Electives:
Students must complete four upper-division electives. These are 5-credit or more-than-5-credit upper-division CSE or CE courses numbered 100-189, CSE 195, or approved courses from the Computational Media electives list. Up to two electives may be replaced by approved upper-division mathematics electives.

Restrictions:
- CSE 115A, CSE 185S, and CSE 185E cannot be used to satisfy one of the four upper-division elective requirements.
- CSE 195 can satisfy the Disciplinary Communication requirement or an upper-division elective, but not both.
- Many upper-division Computer Science courses are restricted to declared Computer Science majors during first-pass or priority enrollment.

Capstone / Comprehensive Requirement:
Students must fulfill the Computer Science exit requirement by either:
1. Passing one of the capstone courses, or
2. Successfully completing a senior thesis.

The capstone course can count toward one of the four required upper-division electives. Many capstone options require additional prerequisites, so advance planning is important.

Disciplinary Communication Requirement:
Students must satisfy the upper-division Disciplinary Communication requirement by either:
1. Passing one of the Disciplinary Communication courses, or
2. Successfully completing a senior thesis.

The DC requirement for the Computer Science B.S. can be satisfied by:
- CSE 115A: Introduction to Software Engineering
- CSE 185E or CSE 185S: Technical Writing for Computer Science and Engineering
- CSE 195: Senior Thesis

DC courses cannot be used to satisfy any of the four upper-division electives, except for CSE 195.

Physics Requirement:
Students must complete one of the following combinations:
- PHYS 5A and PHYS 5B
- PHYS 5A and PHYS 5C
- PHYS 6A and PHYS 6B
- PHYS 6A and PHYS 6C

Note: Physics courses have co-requisite labs required for enrollment. These labs are not part of the Computer Science B.S. major requirements.

Grade Rules:
All courses being applied to Computer Science major requirements must be taken for a letter grade. Grades of P do not count toward major requirements.

Courses with grades of C-, D+, D, or D- earn credit toward graduation, but cannot be used to satisfy a major requirement, a general education requirement, or a prerequisite for another course.

Other Notes:
- Shaded boxes on the chart represent major qualification courses.
- Many graduate courses can also be used to satisfy electives, but students need instructor and department approval.
- The Baskin Engineering major declaration process requires an earlier start than the UCSC Academic/Administrative calendar deadline.
